what this is
Squares A and B send your eye the identical grey. Because B lies in shadow, your visual system discounts the shadow and judges B a lighter surface (Adelson's checker-shadow).
what to do
Drag the shadow off B and watch its value jump apart from A in the readout.
ⓘ about
When B sat in the shadow, it sent your eye exactly the light A did. You saw two different greys anyway. Your visual system discounted the shadow and reported the surface, not the light. Vision is not recording, it is construction.
