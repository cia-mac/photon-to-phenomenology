A gallery of seeing

Photon to Phenomenology interactive phenomena after Stephen Palmer's Vision Science

Each piece is a small instrument. You touch the figure and measure your own visual system, and every one lands on the same fact: vision is not recording, it is construction.

  1. Illusory contours Three notched discs, and a bright triangle that exists only in you. →
  2. The motion aftereffect Spin the spiral, hold still, and the stillness keeps moving. →
  3. The negative afterimage Stare at the teal disc, then look away, and a rose ghost no light is making appears. →
  4. The scintillating grid Count the black dots. There are none. Your retina makes them at the crossings. →
  5. The Ebbinghaus circles Two centre discs, the same size, made to disagree by the company they keep. →
  6. The Cornsweet edge Two halves, one grey. A cusp at the seam paints them apart. Cover it and they fuse. →
  7. The café wall Every row is dead level. Offset tiles and grey mortar tilt them anyway. →
  8. The Ponzo illusion Two bars of one length. Converging rails read as depth and stretch the far one. →
  9. The Müller-Lyer lines Two equal shafts. Fins splayed out or folded in decide which looks longer. →
