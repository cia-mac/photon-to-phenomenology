what this is
Every disc is the same pale grey, yet black dots flash at the crossings you are not looking at. Retinal cells suppressing their bright neighbours (lateral inhibition) overshoot into phantom darkness.
what to do
Let your eyes roam across the grid and watch the crossings flicker. Drag to change the spacing.
ⓘ about
Every disc on this grid is the same pale grey. There is not one black dot anywhere in the image. Your retina sharpens edges by having bright neighbours suppress each other, and at the crossings it overshoots into darkness. The dots are made by you. Vision is not recording, it is construction.
